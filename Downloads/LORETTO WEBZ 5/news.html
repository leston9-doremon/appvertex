03 February 2026 Events
Bidding Adieu: Farewell Programme for Grade X at Loretto Central School

Loretto Central School organised a farewell programme for the students of Grade X Batch 2025–26 in the school auditorium. The occasion was graced by School Manager Rev. Fr. Francis Crasta, Principal Rev. Fr. Jason Vijay Monis, PTA Vice President Mr. Rodney Ivan D'Souza, and Vice Principal Mrs. Anitha Pais.

The programme began with a solemn prayer, setting a reflective and heartfelt tone for the evening. Students of the junior classes presented a variety of cultural performances — songs, dances, and skits — in honour of their departing seniors.

The Grade X students were felicitated and presented with mementos as a token of appreciation for their years of contribution to the school's academic and extracurricular life. The principal addressed the outgoing batch, encouraging them to carry the values of Loretto — Love, Service, and Excellence — throughout their lives.

The event concluded with a heartfelt vote of thanks and a group photograph, leaving both teachers and students with cherished memories of the time spent together at Loretto Central School.

26 January 2026 Celebration
Celebrating India's Glory: 77th Republic Day at Loretto Central School

Loretto Central School celebrated the 77th Republic Day with great patriotism and solemnity on the school grounds. The celebration was graced by the presence of Rev. Fr. Francis Crasta, Manager; Rev. Fr. Jason Vijay Monis, Principal; Sr. Idoline Rodrigues, Headmistress, Loreto Aided Kannada Medium School; Mrs. Anitha Pais, Vice Principal; and Mr. Isaac Vas, Parish Council representative.

The programme commenced with the hoisting of the National Flag, followed by the singing of the National Anthem. Students dressed in tricolour attire presented a colourful march past that drew admiration from all present. Cultural performances highlighting the diversity and unity of India were staged by students from various classes.

The principal's address emphasised the importance of civic responsibility and the sacrifices of freedom fighters who made India what it is today. He urged students to be proud ambassadors of India's rich heritage and to contribute positively to the nation's progress.

The programme concluded with the distribution of sweets and a renewed sense of national pride among all the participants.

Loretto Central School Announces Faculty Recruitment (Academic Year 2026-27)

BANTWAL, Jan 20, 2026 — Loretto Central School, a premier CBSE institution known for its holistic approach to education, has officially opened its faculty recruitment drive for the upcoming academic session. The school is seeking dynamic, innovative, and passionate educators to join its Secondary School wing.

Following its recent transition to the name Loretto Central School, the institution is looking to strengthen its faculty with qualified and experienced teachers across a range of subjects including English, Mathematics, Science, Social Studies, Computer Science, and Physical Education.

Candidates with B.Ed. qualifications and relevant subject expertise are encouraged to apply. The school offers a stimulating work environment, professional development opportunities, and a competitive remuneration package.

Interested candidates may submit their applications along with a detailed resume to the school office or send them via email to info@lorettocentralschool.com. The last date for submission of applications will be communicated through the school's official channels.

Loretto Central School has a proud legacy of academic excellence and is committed to nurturing both students and educators in a spirit of love and service.

07 January 2026 Announcement
Loretto Central School — Official School Name Change Announcement

Loretto, Jan 05 — The management today has announced that the name of the School is officially changed to LORETTO CENTRAL SCHOOL from Loreto English Medium High School, with effect from 06/01/2026.

The CBSE has issued the official letter with Reference No: CBSE/831368/CN-00592-2627/2026-27 approving the name change. Hence the school name and all future correspondence will be in the name of LORETTO CENTRAL SCHOOL.

This change marks a significant milestone in the institution's journey. The management has assured all stakeholders — students, parents, faculty, and staff — that this transition involves only the name, and all academic programmes, affiliations, fee structures, and institutional values remain unchanged.

All official documents, certificates, and communications going forward will bear the new name. Parents of current students need not take any action, as the school will handle the transition in coordination with CBSE and relevant authorities.

The school community welcomes this new chapter with pride and looks forward to continuing its legacy of excellence under the banner of Loretto Central School.

05 January 2026 Achievement
Outstanding Performance in Karate at the National Level Open Karate Competition

Loreto English Medium CBSE High School participated in the National Level Open Karate Competition held at Father Mullers Indoor Stadium, Kankanadi. A total of 18 students from the school took part in the event.

The students performed exceptionally well in both Kata and Kumite categories, winning a total of 8 Gold Medals and 7 Silver Medals — a remarkable achievement that brought tremendous pride and honour to the school.

The school management, principal, and staff congratulated all the participating students on their outstanding performance. Special appreciation was extended to the karate coach and training staff whose guidance and dedication made this success possible.

This achievement reflects the school's commitment to nurturing not just academic excellence, but also the physical and sporting potential of every student. The school continues to support students in participating in state and national level competitions across various disciplines.

Loretto Central School is proud of its young champions and looks forward to even greater achievements in the coming competitions.

02 January 2026 Celebration
Turning the Page to 2026: New Year Celebration at Loreto English Medium High School

Loreto English Medium High School CBSE celebrated the New Year 2026 in the school auditorium. The programme began with a special prayer service during which students read verses from the Holy Bible, the Bhagavad Gita, and the Holy Quran — a beautiful expression of the school's inclusive and harmonious spirit.

The programme was graced by the presence of the School Manager, Rev. Fr. Francis Crasta, the Principal, Rev. Fr. Jason Vijay Monis, and members of the teaching staff. The gathering reflected the school's tradition of beginning every significant occasion with gratitude and prayer.

Students presented cultural performances including songs and dances that set a joyful and hopeful tone for the new year. The Principal addressed the gathering, inspiring students to approach 2026 with renewed energy, dedication, and a commitment to excellence.

He highlighted the school's achievements in 2025 and outlined the goals and aspirations for the year ahead, encouraging students to give their best in academics, sports, and co-curricular activities.

The celebration concluded on a warm note, with students and teachers alike looking forward to a productive and fulfilling year ahead at Loretto.
